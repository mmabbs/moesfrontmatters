## Fitln Sales + Marketing Overview

### Executive Summary
Fitln is an ethical marketplace for fitness and wellness combined with a business management SaaS solution that supports 'fitpreneurs + wellpreneurs'[cite: 4]. The short-term strategy is to populate the marketplace with affiliates' classes + products in order to provide value that attracts customers[cite: 5]. The longer-term strategy is to create awareness of the marketplace to customers in order to generate more transactions, providing value to affiliates[cite: 6]. This creates a virtuous cycle where each side of the business is constantly elevating value for the other[cite: 7].

### Marketing Strategy
1. Fitln's business management software (SaaS) is a strong enough product to do well on its own, but combined with the front-end marketplace, it is an obvious choice for personal trainers, independent instructors, and other professionals who operate sole proprietorship businesses[cite: 9].
2. We will leverage this SaaS solution to onboard the first 100 early-adopter affiliates[cite: 10]. This will help to populate the platform with classes to market to users/customers[cite: 11].
3. As subscriptions will account for a much smaller percentage of revenue than booking fees, we are prepared to waive subscription memberships -where it makes sensein favour of getting more affiliates and their classes/offerings on the platform[cite: 12].
4. User research suggests that if we can get a good cross-section of classes on Fitln, customers are willing to rely on the platform to find their options for fitness and wellness[cite: 13].

### Go to Market Plan
1. Leverage our 95% close rate with affiliates to onboard 100 affiliates in 100 days[cite: 18].
    a. First 100 get a year free subscription[cite: 19].
    b. Focusing on individuals (only 1 decision maker = quicker conversion)[cite: 20].
2. Using e-blasts and appointments with an account manager, train the affiliates to set up and optimize their Fitln accounts[cite: 20].
    a. The better they look, the better we look. We'll be doing regular audits of the storefronts and classes and following up with the affiliates who need our help to improve their presence on Fitln[cite: 21].
    b. The goal is to help them until they're comfortable using Fitln as their primary booking vehicle[cite: 22].
3. Use low-cost marketing techniques to blanket the market area[cite: 23].
    a. Leveraging platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, YouTube, Kijiji, Groupon, ProductHunt, and more[cite: 24].
    b. Create a referral program for Affiliates to bring on more industry professionals[cite: 25].
    c. Because online is currently the preferred medium for fitness classes, we will do our best to draw from all areas of the country[cite: 26, 27].
4. Market via eblast to our 530+ registered users[cite: 28].
5. Close potential partnerships to bring in affiliates + customers[cite: 29].
    a. Canadian Kinesiology Alliance [cite: 30]
    b. StrongerU Fitness [cite: 31]
6. Public Relations Push[cite: 32].
    a. Reach out to all media contacts to pitch Fitln's launch[cite: 33].
    b. Leverage our social enterprise status to create goodwill PR[cite: 34].
    c. Pursue/create speaking opportunities for CEO to promote Fitln[cite: 35].
7. Place ads in CanFitPro and other industry newsletters to attract affiliates[cite: 36].
8. Create Google Ad Words + Facebook campaigns to target conversions from the ideal customer demographic[cite: 37, 38].

### Sales Strategy
1. Establish the budget needed to accomplish business goals[cite: 40].
2. Break that down into monthly and weekly sales goals[cite: 41].
3. Activate our award-winning sales team in hitting every single goal on schedule[cite: 42].
4. Expand the sales team to accelerate affiliate acquisition[cite: 43].
5. Refine focus to shift from personal trainers to small studio gyms[cite: 44].
6. Further focus on therapists will bring a more expensive price point, and better booking fee revenue[cite: 45].
