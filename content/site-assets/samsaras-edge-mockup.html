Design Philosophy

Narrative Pillars

The core principles guiding story decisions, ensuring thematic coherence across every piece of content.

Externalized Internal Conflict

Every dungeon manifests a psychological wound from the couple's history. The "baggage" isn't metaphorical—players literally fight their repressed arguments.

Comedy as Coping Mechanism

Dark humor isn't decoration—it's how Sam and Sara deflect from genuine vulnerability. The funniest moments should also be the most painful.

Systemic Critique Through Play

The AI-governed city isn't dystopian—it's uncomfortably familiar. Players discover that "algorithmic compatibility" mirrors real dating app optimization.

Systems Design

Core Mechanics

How narrative themes translate into gameplay systems. Each mechanic is designed to create meaningful story beats through player interaction.

Mechanic Type Narrative Function
Dual-Deck System Core Sara and Sam each have separate decks representing their emotional "arsenal." Synergies require understanding your partner's perspective.
Compatibility Meter Progression Global score that shifts based on dialogue choices and combat cooperation. Endings vary dramatically based on final score.
Memory Fragments Core Collectible flashbacks unlocked by exploring dungeons. Each reveals backstory while providing permanent card upgrades.
Baggage Cards Progression Negative cards added to decks representing unresolved issues. Can be purged through specific encounters or confrontation.
Couple's Counseling Social Between-run hub where players allocate "therapy points" to unlock dialogue options and relationship perks.
Development Notes

Process Timeline

Concept & Prototyping

Q2 2024

Initial concept emerged from a game jam prompt: "two players, one controller." Early prototypes explored asymmetric cooperation mechanics before settling on the dual-deck deckbuilder format. The relationship metaphor clicked when we realized deck synergy could represent emotional compatibility.

Vertical Slice

Q4 2024

Built a single dungeon (the "First Apartment" memory) with 30 unique cards. Playtests revealed that players loved the comedy but weren't connecting with the emotional core. Rewrote the magistrate scene to make stakes clearer—Sam and Sara's marriage literally depends on dungeon completion.

Current Focus

2025

Expanding content to 5 dungeons with ~150 cards. Writing focus is on memory fragments that reveal backstory through environmental storytelling. Exploring partnership opportunities with narrative-focused publishers.

Reflections

What I've Learned

What Worked

Comedy as a trojan horse for sincerity. Players who came for the joke about passive-aggressive card attacks stayed for the genuine exploration of communication breakdown. The tonal balance of The Lobster—absurdist premise, emotionally devastating execution—proved to be the right model.

What I'd Do Differently

Earlier integration of narrative and mechanics design. Writing character bios before understanding card synergy led to rewrites when Sara's "control" archetype clashed with her deck's support-focused cards. In future projects, I'd prototype mechanics and narrative beats in parallel.

Open Questions

How much player agency in relationship outcomes? Early feedback split between players who wanted to "save" the marriage and those who wanted the option to consciously uncouple. Currently exploring whether multiple valid endings dilute the thematic message or strengthen it.
